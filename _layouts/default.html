Temoor Tanveer

I am a Research Assistant at Qatar Computing Research Institute, where my focus is on LLM safety and alignment. The broad focus of my research here is detecting fraudulent financial transactions through LLM and machine learning methods.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked as a Research Intern at the Networking Systems Lab, where I co-developed an open-source federated learning testbed. This modularized distributed system utilized edge computing (Androids, VMs) to train ML models while preserving privacy. Our research focused on studying the effects of non-IID data, model complexity, churn, and network settings on model accuracy and efficiency, resulting in three publications. The project is available at: RealFL Repository.

I served as a Teaching Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University, supporting multiple courses including the graduate-level cloud computing course at the TEELLab. Additionally, I provided teaching support for Great Theoretical Ideas in CS, Imperative Programming, and Intro to Programming.
